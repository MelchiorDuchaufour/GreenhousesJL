==============================================================================
SLIDE 11e — WHY STANGHELLINI, NOT FAO-PM
Arguments, source quotes, and side-by-side demonstrations
==============================================================================

1. ARGUMENT IN ONE LINE
-----------------------
FAO Penman-Monteith is a sound energy-balance backbone, but every one of its
calibrated constants assumes "open-field reference grass". Five of those
assumptions are quantitatively violated inside a greenhouse; a sixth
(empirical Kc correction) cannot recover them because they are coupled.
Stanghellini (1987) rebuilt each sub-model for greenhouse conditions.
GreenhousesJL implements her form, with modern additions (Medlyn rs,
multilayer radiosity).

2. SOURCE QUOTES (Stanghellini 1987, Ch. 1)
-------------------------------------------
"A greenhouse crop has peculiarities which rule out the simple transcription
of experimental techniques developed for crops in a more natural environment.
The resistance to the vertical exchange of heat for a field crop is normally
estimated by the assumption that the canopy is immersed in a boundary layer
of wind, characterized by a well-defined (logarithmic) profile of the wind
speed. THIS ASSUMPTION IS OBVIOUSLY UNTRUE for a canopy enclosed in a
greenhouse."

"As sources of radiation are present within a greenhouse canopy, such an
experimental method [above-canopy Rn - ground flux] CANNOT BE APPLIED HERE."

"Obviously, all these constraints make the combination method UNSUITABLE, IN
THIS FORM, for the present purpose of determining the actual transpiration
rate of a greenhouse crop."

3. FIVE SIDE-BY-SIDE DIFFERENCES (demonstrations with numbers)
==============================================================================

---- (1) AERODYNAMIC RESISTANCE r_a -------------------------------------------
FAO assumes:   r_a = 208 / u_2           [Allen 1998, eq.4 / FAO-56 box 4]
                                          valid only for u_2 in a log wind
                                          profile over 0.12 m grass
Indoor fact:   u in greenhouse ≈ 0.1 to 0.3 m/s. Plug in u_2 = 0.1 → r_a =
               2080 s/m, i.e. transpiration collapses by 30×. The log-profile
               formula simply does not apply to a confined volume.

Stanghellini: Nusselt-number formulation, free + forced convection mixed.
               r_a = rho·cp·l / (Nu·lambda_air)   with Nu = f(Re, Gr, Pr)
               l = characteristic leaf size. Calibrated in a greenhouse from
               heated-leaf experiments (her Ch. 2).

Takeaway:     FAO gives 3-30× wrong r_a indoors.

---- (2) STOMATAL / CANOPY RESISTANCE r_s -------------------------------------
FAO assumes:   r_s,reference = 70 s/m  (fixed, reference grass).
               Hidden inside the 0.34·u_2 term:
               rs/ra ≈ 70 / (208/u_2) = 70·u_2/208 → the 0.34·u_2.
               r_s is NOT a parameter you can adjust; it's baked in.

Indoor fact:   Tomato r_s is ~50 s/m midday but collapses to 5000 s/m at
               night when PAR < 5 umol/m2/s (stomata close). Dependence on
               VPD and CO_2 is also strong (factor 2-3 over daylight hours).

Stanghellini/ours: r_s = r_s(PAR, CO_2, VPD) via Medlyn (2011):
               g_s = g_0 + (1 + g_1/sqrt(VPD)) · A_n/C_a
               r_s = 1/g_s
               g_0, g_1 species-specific, fitted from leaf-level data.

Takeaway:     Fixed 70 s/m cannot capture day/night or humidity/CO_2 effects.

---- (3) NET RADIATION R_n ---------------------------------------------------
FAO assumes:   R_n measured above a closed canopy, no obstruction between the
               sun and the grass, no structure overhead.
               Typically R_n = (1 - alpha_can) · R_s ↓ - R_sky ↑

Indoor fact:   Solar is attenuated by cover (tau ≈ 0.85), possibly also by
               screen (tau ≈ 0.5), possibly by PV panel (tau_PV). Canopy sees
               a multi-bounce PAR + NIR distribution (slide 08c). Longwave:
               cover radiates DOWN onto the canopy; screen, PV, floor also
               participate.

GreenhousesJL: R_n,can built from
               - shortwave cascade (slide 08c): PAR/NIR split, multi-layer
                 tau_rho up/down bounces, cover + canopy + floor;
               - LW radiosity enclosure (slide 08b): N-surface network with
                 view factors from 3D geometry, no above-canopy fiction.

Takeaway:     FAO's R_n proxy misses both shortwave redistribution and
               indoor LW exchange with the structure.

---- (4) INDOOR VPD FEEDBACK -------------------------------------------------
FAO assumes:   VPD = e*(T_air) - e_a, computed from the outside-air met
               station (daily). Instantaneous value is not a state variable.

Indoor fact:   Transpiration pumps vapor INTO a nearly closed enclosure.
               Indoor RH rises toward 95-100 % within minutes when vents
               are closed, so VPD_indoor → 0 → transpiration damps itself.
               This feedback is the dominant dynamic on humid days.

GreenhousesJL: VPD is derived from the indoor vapor balance (slide 11b) at
               every timestep:
               d(VP_air)/dt = (1/V_air) · (MV_CanAir - MV_AirCov - MV_AirOut
                              - ...). Transpiration then sees VPD_indoor(t).

Takeaway:     Without the feedback, FAO systematically OVER-ESTIMATES ET on
               humid and/or poorly-ventilated days.

---- (5) TIME SCALE AND CLOSURE ----------------------------------------------
FAO assumes:   Daily integral (or hourly, with different constants: eq.53).
               Coefficients 0.408, 900, 0.34 are DERIVED for the daily step.

Indoor fact:   Climate controllers operate at second-to-minute step. Humidity
               and CO_2 setpoints, vent-opening, and CHP dispatch all react
               within 2-5 minutes.

GreenhousesJL: Sub-hourly ODE integration (Tsit5), RH and VPD computed from
               states; cover condensation at night is closed back into the
               cover energy balance (slide 11a / slide 08). Water loop closes.

Takeaway:     FAO daily form is not usable for controller design;
               Stanghellini form is.

5. HOW TO DEFEND (one paragraph, thesis-ready)
==============================================================================

"GreenhousesJL implements the adaptation of the Penman-Monteith-Rijtema
combination method developed by Stanghellini (1987, Ch. 2-3) specifically
for greenhouse conditions, with a Nusselt-based aerodynamic resistance, a
dynamic stomatal resistance from Medlyn et al. (2011), a net radiation term
built from our own shortwave cascade and multi-surface longwave radiosity
enclosure, and indoor VPD as a state variable that damps transpiration near
saturation. The FAO-56 form (Allen et al. 1998) retains the Penman-Monteith
backbone but uses reference-grass constants that Stanghellini explicitly
flagged as unsuitable for greenhouses; in particular, the 208/u_2 aerodynamic
resistance fails at indoor wind speeds below 0.5 m/s, and the 70 s/m fixed
surface resistance cannot capture stomatal closure at night."
