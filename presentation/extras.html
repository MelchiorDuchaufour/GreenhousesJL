02

Sun model: three levers

Bare-greenhouse validation showed the cover / floor shortwave path drives nearly all the day-time energy balance. Three levers were added on top of the Modelica baseline.

1. PAR / NIR spectral split

  • Broadband single-pass (Modelica default) overestimates NIR absorption by leaves (canopy PAR > NIR absorptivity).
  • Julia tracks PAR and NIR separately, each with its own (τ, ρ, α) pair on cover, floor, canopy.

2. Fresnel τ(θ)

  • Constant τ over-transmits at high incidence, problematic at winter latitudes (sun elevation < 25°).
  • Fresnel correction with n=1.526, diffuse factor 0.88. Low-sun transmission drops to ~50 % at θ=75°.

3. Multi-bounce cover, floor

  • Single-pass cover ignores the fraction of diffuse light that bounces back up and gets reabsorbed.
  • Julia iterates the cover, floor exchange to steady state, adds ~3, 5 % on realistic ρ_flr.

Shortwave partition at the cover

Ieff = Iglob·((1, cPV) + cPV·τPV)

RCov = (αcov,PAR·ηPAR + αcov,NIR·ηNIR)·Ieff

Rt,PAR = Ieff·ηPAR·τcov,PAR·(1, ηglob,air)
RNIR = Ieff·ηNIR·τcov,NIR·(1, ηglob,air)

RFlr = Rt,PAR·exp(, K1·LAI)·(1, ρFlr,PAR) + RNIR·αFlr,NIR
RAir = ηglob,air·Ieff

→ toggle set_solar_model!(:standard) vs set_solar_model!(:ep_broadband) to switch between the two treatments at runtime.

03

Sun model: standard vs broadband

Same bare-greenhouse setup as the cross-weather benchmark, 7-day December, 14 000 m² Venlo, closed envelope. Only the solar kernel changes.

  • standard: PAR/NIR, Fresnel τ(θ), multi-bounce.
  • ep_broadband: single-band, constant τ, no bounce.

Daytime peaks diverge by 1, 3 °C, night tracks match within 0.2 °C. Bias grows with solar, biggest in Almería.

→ PAR/NIR split + Fresnel carry most of the improvement. Multi-bounce alone is second-order.

08

PV panel energy balance

Cpv · dTpv/dt = + Qsol,pv − Qcnv,top − Qcnv,gap − Qrad,sky − Qrad,cov − Pelec
PV panel energy balance

Tilted panel above the cover

Panel temperature drives electrical efficiency (η(T)), longwave exchange with sky and glass, and convective coupling through the air gap.

All six terms wired to the interior radiosity enclosure (slide 10b, main deck) and to the cover energy balance.

Term 1 of 6
Qsol,pv
W · solar absorbed by panel

Qsol,pv = αpv(θ) · Iglob · Apv

Fresnel angular absorption. Opaque: α≈0.90. Semi-transparent: α≈0.55 with τ=0.4 passing through.

Term 2 of 6
Qcnv,top
W · panel → outside convection

Qcnv,top = hw(u,φ) · (Tpv − Tout) · A

Sharples & Charlesworth (1998) wind-directional hw on tilted PV. Typical 10−25 W/m²K.

Term 3 of 6 updated
Qcnv,gap
W · panel → glass through air cavity

Qcnv,gap = hHollands(φ,ΔT) · (Tpv − Tcov) · A

Hollands et al. (1976) correlation for inclined enclosed cavities. Feeds back into the cover balance (slide 10 term 2).

Term 4 of 6
Qrad,sky
W · panel → sky IR

Qrad,sky = εpv·Fsky(φ)·σ·A·(Tpv4 − Tsky4)

εpv=0.90 (Faiman 2008). Fsky=(1+cosφ)/2 for tilt φ. Dominant night loss, drives Tpv below Tout.

Term 5 of 6
Qrad,cov
W · panel → cover IR

Qrad,cov = εpv,cov·(1−Fsky)·σ·A·(Tpv4 − Tcov4)

Two-surface grey-body (εpv,cov≈0.82). Shows up as an incoming term on the cover balance (slide 10 term 3, Qrad,int) through the enclosure.

Term 6 of 6
Pelec
W · extracted electrical power

Pelec = ηref · [1 − β·(Tpv − 25°C)] · Iglob · A

ηref≈0.20, β≈0.004 /K (crystalline Si). Energy extracted as electricity reduces the panel heat budget, lowers Tpv, and feeds the grid on the webapp HUD.
