## FSPM vs simplified crop models (1D vs 3D comparison)

- **Sarlikioti, V., de Visser, P.H.B. & Marcelis, L.F.M.** (2011). Exploring the spatial distribution of light interception and photosynthesis of canopies by means of a functional–structural plant model. *Annals of Botany*, 107(5), 875–883. [doi:10.1093/aob/mcr006](https://doi.org/10.1093/aob/mcr006)

- **Sarlikioti, V., de Visser, P.H.B. & Marcelis, L.F.M.** (2020). Quantifying the importance of a realistic tomato (*Solanum lycopersicum*) leaflet shape for 3-D light modelling. *Annals of Botany*, 126(4), 661–670. [doi:10.1093/aob/mcz205](https://doi.org/10.1093/aob/mcz205)

  > Key finding: simplified leaf shapes (ellipse, rectangle) deviate only **0.5–1.9%** from realistic shapes for light absorption in tomato, and **0.3–0.9%** at canopy level.

- **Li, Y. et al.** (2021). How does structure matter? Comparison of canopy photosynthesis using one- and three-dimensional light models: a case study using greenhouse cucumber canopies. *in silico Plants*, 3(2), diab031. [doi:10.1093/insilicoplants/diab031](https://doi.org/10.1093/insilicoplants/diab031)

  > Key finding: with 3D-calibrated extinction coefficient *k*, the simple Beer-Lambert model provides **equivalent accuracy** to 3D ray-tracing for long-term greenhouse simulations. *k* varies by 0.2 depending on row geometry.

- **Kang, M. et al.** (2023). Functional–Structural Plant Model "GreenLab": A State-of-the-Art Review. *Plant Phenomics*, 0118. [doi:10.34133/plantphenomics.0118](https://doi.org/10.34133/plantphenomics.0118)

## Numerical integration & implementation

- **Tsitouras, Ch.** (2011). Runge–Kutta pairs of order 5(4) satisfying only the first quadrature condition. *Computers & Mathematics with Applications*, 62(2), 770–775. [doi:10.1016/j.camwa.2011.06.002](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.camwa.2011.06.002)

- **Bezanson, J., Edelman, A., Karpinski, S. & Shah, V.B.** (2017). Julia: A Fresh Approach to Numerical Computing. *SIAM Review*, 59(1), 65–98. [doi:10.1137/141000671](https://doi.org/10.1137/141000671). Preprint: [arxiv:1411.1607](https://arxiv.org/abs/1411.1607)

- **U.S. Department of Energy** (2024). *EnergyPlus Engineering Reference — Climate, Sky and Solar Calculations.* [Online documentation](https://bigladdersoftware.com/epx/docs/25-1/engineering-reference/climate-calculations.html)
